About Nakai San

The Legendary japanese car tuner you should know about

One well-known auto tuner you should be familiar with is Akira Nakai, who founded the Porsche tuning business RAUH-Welt BEGRIFF (RWB). When he was younger, Nakai san was a Japanese auto racer. However, he didn't begin to tune cars until after he retired. He then opened a car tuning shop in Kashiwa City, Chiba Prefecture, Tokyo, Japan, called Rauh-Welt Begriff (RWB), which translates to "to transform a car into a wilder and rugged state" in German. The garage RWB specializes in tuning vintage Porsche models, such the 930, 946, 993, and so on. "The Porsche car itself is in great appearance and performance, while I am just expanding its potential and manufacturability." , remarked Nakai San. In his remarks, Nakai san encapsulated His reasons for his fondness for Porsche and his more than two decades of tuning the vehicle. Every car has a spirit of its own, partially given by the owner and partially inherent from creation. Thus, the owners have given me every Porsche that I have personally tuned." Nakai san assembled the entire kit and chassis, including the binding angle and tilt angle adjustments, by hand. In addition, he will manually measure, touch, and polish every piece of trim. The finest work has been made possible by the enthusiasm and focus of Akira Nakai, the master of tuning. The artwork Stella, which strives for perpetual perfection, has been on exhibit at Tokyo Auto in 2009. This is how the RWB tuning journey began to take the world by storm.

RWB Porche

Love ’em or loathe ’em, you can’t look away from these massively modified 911s.

The simplest definition of a RWB Porsche is a widebody Porsche 911 that has been modified by renowned Japanese tuner Akira Nakai. However, the actual solution is much more nuanced. Particularly among the ardent Porsche enthusiasts, these heavily customized 911s with their oversized wings, deep-dish wheels, exposed rivets, and fender flares make RWB Porsches very divisive and contentious automobiles. RWB-modified vehicles are perceived as either blasphemous, caricatured monstrosities made by a renegade car-tooner, or as rolling works of art crafted by a master car tuner. However, one thing that all parties can agree on is that RWB Porsches will always spark controversy in a society that is desperate for its next viral Instagram picture.
